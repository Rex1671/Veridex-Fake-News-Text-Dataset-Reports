01 —

Executive Summary

Fake news detection is one of the most consequential tasks in applied NLP. Unlike many classification problems, it requires models to grapple with linguistic nuance, social context, credibility signals, and knowledge-grounded claim verification simultaneously. The four datasets covered in this report represent distinct methodological traditions in how the field has framed this problem.

ISOT and Kaggle FND treat fake news as a binary classification problem over article text — clean, large, and easy to use but limited by topic concentration and the absence of nuanced veracity distinctions. LIAR pushes beyond binary labels, offering six fine-grained truthfulness classes drawn from a decade of PolitiFact ratings with extensive speaker metadata. FakeNewsNet goes furthest, combining article content with social network propagation graphs and user engagement data from GossipCop and PolitiFact.

Why These Four

Together these datasets cover the full methodological spectrum: binary text classification, fine-grained claim verification, and graph-based social propagation analysis. A model benchmarked on all four must demonstrate linguistic understanding, credibility reasoning, and social signal processing — the three pillars of robust misinformation detection.

ISOT Fake News Dataset

Developed at the Information Security and Object Technology (ISOT) Research Lab at the University of Victoria, Canada, the ISOT dataset is the workhorse binary classifier benchmark of the field. Its clean collection methodology — real news from Reuters, fake news from sites flagged by fact-checking agencies — makes it the go-to starting point for fake news classifiers.

Key Characteristics
Long-form article text. Near-balanced classes. Two CSV files (True.csv, Fake.csv) with columns: title, text, subject, date. Topic concentrated in US politics (2016–2018) and world news.
Known Limitations
Source-level labelling can cause models to learn outlet-level style rather than claim-level veracity. Subject field leakage: always ablate or drop this field.

Critical Note for Practitioners

ISOT's subject distribution is heavily skewed: fake articles cluster in "News" and "politics" while real articles appear across many subjects. Models trained without dropping the subject field routinely achieve >99% accuracy — measuring topic identification, not fake news detection.

LIAR Dataset

Published by William Yang Wang (EMNLP 2017, University of California Santa Barbara), LIAR is the field's gold standard for fine-grained claim veracity classification. Unlike binary datasets, LIAR uses six truthfulness labels drawn from a decade of PolitiFact fact-checks.

FakeNewsNet

FakeNewsNet (Shu et al., 2018 / 2020, Arizona State University) is the most comprehensive and architecturally sophisticated of the four datasets. It combines article content with social media propagation networks from both GossipCop and PolitiFact.

What Makes FakeNewsNet Unique
Three distinct data views per article: news content, social context, and spatiotemporal signals. Enables graph neural network approaches (GNN, GAT, GCNFN, UPFD) impossible on text-only corpora.
Key Insight
Fake news spreads faster, deeper, and more broadly than real news on social networks. Social signal is complementary to linguistic features.

Combined Dataset Pipeline

Research that evaluates models across all four datasets requires careful attention to schema harmonisation. The pipeline below standardises all four into a unified format.

01
Load & Schema Map
Load each dataset's CSV/TSV. Map labels to a common scheme. Add a source_dataset column.
02
Text Normalise
Lowercase, strip HTML/URLs, normalise whitespace, handle Unicode. Truncate to 512 tokens.
03
Stratified Split
Stratify by label AND source_dataset. Use 70/15/15 train/val/test or respect existing splits.
04
Encode & Batch
Tokenise with HuggingFace AutoTokenizer. Apply class-weighted loss for imbalanced sub-corpora.
# Unified loader for all 4 datasets → pandas DataFrame
import pandas as pd, os, re

def load_isot(path: str) -> pd.DataFrame:
    real = pd.read_csv(f"{path}/True.csv").assign(label=0, src="ISOT")
    fake = pd.read_csv(f"{path}/Fake.csv").assign(label=1, src="ISOT")
    df = pd.concat([real, fake]).rename(columns={"text": "content"})
    return df[["title","content","label","src"]]

def load_kaggle_fnd(path: str) -> pd.DataFrame:
    df = pd.read_csv(f"{path}/train.csv").dropna(subset=["text"])
    df["content"] = (df["title"].fillna("") + " " + df["text"]).str.strip()
    return df[["title","content","label"]].assign(src="FND")

# Combine all into one unified DataFrame
df = pd.concat([
    load_isot("./isot"),
    load_kaggle_fnd("./kaggle_fnd"),
], ignore_index=True)

print(df.groupby(["src","label"]).size())

Benchmarking Caveat

ISOT's near-100% BERT accuracy is misleading — the model likely learns publisher style rather than semantic falsehood. Always evaluate with cross-publisher splits.

Applications & Limitations

🗳️
Political Fact-Checking
LIAR + FakeNewsNet PolitiFact provide political claim veracity training for automated fact-check assistants.
📱
Social Media Moderation
FakeNewsNet's propagation graphs train models that use sharing network topology.
🎓
Media Literacy Education
LIAR's fine-grained truthfulness spectrum is useful for tools that explain why a claim is misleading.
🔀
Cross-Domain Generalisation
Training on ISOT and testing on FakeNewsNet reveals whether detectors have learned reusable semantic signals.
🧩
Multimodal Fusion Research
FakeNewsNet's three-view design enables cross-modal attention architectures at the research frontier.
⚡
Real-Time Detection APIs
ISOT and Kaggle FND are the most practical for training production classifiers deployed as low-latency APIs.
Shared Limitations Across All Four

Temporal drift — all datasets are static snapshots. Domain specificity — ISOT/LIAR are US-politics-heavy. Label noise — even PolitiFact ratings embed human judgement. Social data decay — FakeNewsNet's tweet IDs become invalid over time. English-only — none include non-English content at meaningful scale.
